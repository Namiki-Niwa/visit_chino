What's in Chino City ?

Nagano Prefecture's Chino City is a naturally abundant area nestled amidst picturesque mountains and clear streams. Visitors can revel in the beauty of nature throughout the four seasons. The city boasts hot spring resorts, offering opportunities for relaxation and rejuvenation. Chino City is also rich in historical landmarks and local culinary delights, providing visitors with a diverse array of attractions to explore and enjoy.

Summer

Summer in Japan is characterized by hot and humid weather. It's a season of vibrant festivals like Tanabata and Obon, where traditional dances and fireworks displays light up the night sky. Many people escape the heat by heading to the mountains or beaches, enjoying activities like hiking, swimming, and summer festivals.

Autumn

Autumn in Japan is famous for its stunning foliage, known as "koyo." The leaves turn vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow, painting the landscape with breathtaking colors. It's a time for leisurely strolls through parks and gardens, enjoying the crisp air and the beauty of nature. Autumn is also the season for harvest festivals and delicious seasonal foods like chestnuts and mushrooms.

Winter

Winter in Japan brings cold temperatures and sometimes heavy snowfall, especially in the northern regions. It's a season for enjoying winter sports like skiing and snowboarding in popular resorts such as Hokkaido and Nagano. Winter illuminations light up cities, creating a magical atmosphere, and people warm up with hot springs (onsen) and traditional hotpot dishes like nabe.

Public Transportation

Nagano Prefecture, Chino City, is accessible by a direct train from Tokyo. Take the Limited Express Azusa from Tokyo JR Shinjuku Station for a two-hour journey. Trains depart every hour.
